If you want a quick link to a plant, that's probably the site to use.

    You can craft a link that will open a given set of plants on either this page or the other - by way of example, if you wanted a link that would open the plants Agrimonia gryposepala, Berberis thunbergii, and Silene vulgaris when the page loaded, you would use the link http://mainwest.zxq.net/pbc_flora/pbc_flora.html?plant=agrgry,berthu,silvul where “?plant=agrgry,berthu,silvul” has been appended to the standard link and the codes come from the first three letters of each word in the species name. The same format works on the alternate site: http://mainwest.zxq.net/pbc_flora/pbc_plant.php?plant=agrgry,berthu,silvul. You can open up to 25 plants this way using the scientific names that appear on this page.

  • 9/19/10: Two new pieces of information have been added to individual plants, when available.
    • The Wetland Indicator of a plant is, to quote http://wisplants.uwsp.edu/wetind.html, a code that "represents the estimated probability (likelihood) of a species occurring in wetlands versus non-wetlands in the region." The codes and their meaning are as follows (with more details at the link):
      • Obligate Wetland (OBL). Occur almost always (estimated probability >99%) under natural conditions in wetlands.
      • Facultative Wetland (FACW). Usually occur in wetlands (estimated probability 67%-99%), but occasionally found in non-wetlands.
      • Facultative (FAC). Equally likely to occur in wetlands or non-wetlands (estimated probability 34%-66%).
      • Facultative Upland (FACU). Usually occur in non-wetlands (estimated probability 67%-99%), but occasionally found in wetlands (estimated probability 1%-33%).
      • Obligate Upland (UPL). Occur in wetlands in another region, but occur almost always (estimated probability >99%) under natural conditions in non-wetlands in the region specified.
    • The C value, or Coefficient of Conservatism, of a plant is, to quote http://wisplants.uwsp.edu/WFQA.html, "A number on a scale from 0 to 10 that represents an estimated probability that a plant species is likely to occur in a landscape relatively unaltered from what is believed to be a pre-settlement condition. A C of 0 indicates the probability is almost 0, while a C of 10 indicates the plant is almost certain to be found only in an undegraded natural community. Introduced plants were not part of the pre-settlement flora, so no coefficient is assigned to them." See the link for more detailed information.

    The plant list can be sorted by either value, which, in the case of the wetland indicator, should help in identification: a plant listed as OBL (obligate wetland) is very unlikely to be found in the prairie or on the hill, for example.

    The data come from the Robert W. Freckmann Herbarium, University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point Wisconsin Plants web site (http://wisplants.uwsp.edu).
